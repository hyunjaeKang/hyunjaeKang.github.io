Bio-Cell Manipulation

Project title Development of Bio-Cell Manipulation System
Period 2003 – 2004
Supervisor Dr. B. Kim and Dr. B. Ju
Funding IMC (Intelligent Microsystem Center)

Purpose (abstract):

In this research, a microrobotic biomanipulation system based on the hybrid manipulation process to handle individual zebrafish embryos is presented. It consists of a microrobotic injector with cellular force sensing capabilities and a visual tracking system. First, the visual recognition system is constructed to autonomously position individual cells in a desired position under an optical microscope. In addition, the force sensor integrated microinjection pipette is developed to give force-feedback to the operator as well as to characterize mechanical properties of cell membranes. Using the semi-autonomous biomanipulation platform, zebrafish embryo manipulation process is carefully planned to recognize, transport, track, fix, and penetrate individual cells sequentially. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed biomanipulation platform can contribute greatly to the improvement of speed and success rates. Further research efforts on improving the efficiency and robustness of the biomanipulation platform and biological tests are on-going.

My role in this project:

  • Development of cell manipulation system for cell handling.
  • Development of graphic user interface for cell manipulation system.
  • Development of pattern matching algorithm-using image processing for cell auto-detection.
  • Development of the force sensor using PVDF for cellular force measurement

Related tools:

  • Software: Visual C++, Open GL, Direct X 9.0 SDK (Direct show).
